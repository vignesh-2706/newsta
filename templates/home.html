Skin CAncer

What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells which most often develops on skin exposed to the sun. But this common form of cancer can also occur on areas of your skin not ordinarily exposed to sunlight. 

Checking your skin for suspicious changes can help detect skin cancer at its earliest stages. Early detection of skin cancer gives you the greatest chance for successful skin cancer treatment.

Symptoms

Skin cancers aren't all identical, and they may not cause many initial symptoms. Still, unusual changes to your skin can be a warning sign for the different types of cancer. Being alert for changes to your skin may help you get a diagnosis earlier. Watch out for a new skin lesion, unusual growth, bump, sore, scaly patch, or dark spot develops and doesn't disappear.

ASYMMETRY

The lesion is not symmetrical, meaning that the two halves of the lseion are not identical.

BORDER

The lesions have uneven edges or irregurality in the border. They are not smooth or even edged.

COLOR

It has an unusual colour, such as white, pink, black, blue, or red. It could also have more than one color within a lesion.

DIAMETER

The lesions have a larger diameter, usually more than 6mm or 1/4 inch.

Skin Cancer Detection

Why Early Detection is important?

Skin cancer is usually visible on the outside, unlike the other cancers which develop inside the body. Hence the skin examination is vital both at home by yourself and the dermatologist. 

Detection
early detection saves lives

Learning to look for warning signs on you self-examination of skin and if anything unusual, then getting it checked by the dermatologist as soon as possible is recommended.

Causes and Risk Factors of Skin Cancer

Skin cancer occurs when mutations develop in the DNA of your skin cells. These mutations cause skin cells to grow uncontrollably and form a mass of cancer cells. Many of the causes of skin cancer are unclear. Most moles don’t turn into melanomas, and researchers aren’t sure why some do.However, risk factors may make you more prone to developing skin cancers, like melanoma.

UV Light Exposure

The exposure to UV light is a significant risk factor for many types of skin cancer. Sources of UV light exposure include the sun, tanning beds, sun lamps.
UV rays damage your skin cells. When the damage causes an overgrowth of cells, skin cancer develops.

Light skin, light hair, and freckling

People with lighter skin are at greater risk of skin cancer, especially those with:
naturally red or blonde hair, blue or green eyes, light skin that tends to burn or freckle easily in the sun

Weakened immune system

When other diseases or health treatments cause damage to your immune system, the chances are higher that you will develop skin cancer. If you undergo chemotherapy or take certain medication that weakend the immune system.

Older age




Even though skin cancer is seen in children and young adults, it is most common in people over 30 years old.
